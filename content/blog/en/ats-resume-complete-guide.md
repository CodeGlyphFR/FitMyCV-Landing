## What Is an ATS and Why Should You Care in 2026?

An ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software used by companies to manage the entire hiring process. It's the first gatekeeper between your resume and a human recruiter.

Here's how it works: **parsing** (text extraction) → **matching** (keyword comparison with the job posting) → **scoring** (compatibility rating) → **shortlisting** (ranking top candidates).

The numbers are striking: **75% of resumes are rejected before a human ever reads them**. 99% of Fortune 500 companies use an ATS, and the adoption rate among mid-sized companies continues to climb. Whether you're applying through Workday, Greenhouse, Taleo, SmartRecruiters, or any other platform, your resume goes through an automated filter.

In 2026, these systems are more sophisticated than ever. Ignoring them means applying into the void.

### How an ATS Reads Your Resume, Step by Step

Think of it as a robot reading your resume through a very specific lens:

1. **Text extraction** — The ATS converts your file into plain text, stripping all visual formatting.
2. **Section identification** — It looks for standard headings (Experience, Education, Skills) to structure the information.
3. **Keyword extraction** — Technical skills, certifications, and job titles are isolated and indexed.
4. **Scoring** — Each keyword is compared against the job posting. More matches mean a higher score.
5. **Recruiter presentation** — Your profile appears in a dashboard with your score. Recruiters only see the top-ranked resumes.

Think of it like an internal search engine: if your resume doesn't contain the right keywords in the right places, it simply doesn't appear in the results.

## What an ATS Actually Analyzes

### Keywords and Technical Skills

This is the number one criterion. The ATS compares the skills on your resume with those in the job posting. Two types of matching exist:

- **Hard match** — The keyword must appear exactly as written ("Python," "SAP," "Excel").
- **Semantic match** — Modern ATS systems recognize synonyms. "Project management" and "project leadership" may be treated as equivalent.

Don't rely on semantic matching — not all systems support it. The safest approach is using the exact terms from the job posting. [AI can save you significant time](/blog/tailor-ats-resume-ai) in identifying the right keywords to include.

### Work Experience and Career History

The ATS parses your employment dates to calculate years of experience. It also detects career gaps and compares your job titles with the target role. A title close to the one in the posting significantly boosts your score.

### Education and Certifications

ATS systems recognize degrees, university names, and professional certifications (PMP, AWS Solutions Architect, Google Analytics, CPA…). List them with their full official titles to maximize recognition.

## Formats Accepted and Rejected by ATS Systems

| Criterion | Compatible | Incompatible |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **File format** | .docx, text-based PDF | Scanned PDF, .jpg, .png |
| **Layout** | Single column, linear | Tables, multiple columns |
| **Fonts** | Calibri, Arial, Times New Roman | Decorative, handwritten fonts |
| **Headings** | Standard section titles (H2, H3) | Word headers/footers |
| **Visual elements** | Simple bullets, bold text | Charts, skill bars, icons, photos |

## 5 Golden Rules for an ATS-Friendly Resume

**1. Tailor your resume to each job posting.** A generic resume scores low. Use the specific keywords from each listing. You can [check your compatibility score](/blog/resume-match-score-explained) before submitting.

**2. Use a machine-readable format.** Text-based PDF or .docx, single column, no tables. See our [ATS formatting guide](/blog/ats-compatible-resume-format) for details.

**3. Include the right keywords naturally.** Place technical skills in a dedicated section AND within the context of your work experience. The ATS checks both.

**4. Use standard section headings.** "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Languages" — save creativity for the content, not the section titles.

## FAQ

**Does a resume with a photo pass ATS screening?**
The ATS simply ignores the photo — it doesn't cause a technical problem. However, it takes up space that could contain keywords. In most countries, photos aren't required; prioritize content.

**What's the best file format?**
A text-based PDF (created from Word or a text editor) offers the best balance between ATS compatibility and layout preservation. The .docx format is also well supported.

**Do ATS systems read cover letters?**
Most ATS systems only analyze the resume. Some newer systems are beginning to scan cover letters, but the resume remains the primary document for scoring.

**How do I know if my resume was rejected by an ATS?**
If you regularly apply without hearing back — even for positions that match your profile — that's a strong signal. Test your resume with an ATS analysis tool to identify what's blocking it.
