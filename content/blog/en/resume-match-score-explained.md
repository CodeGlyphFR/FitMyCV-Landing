## What Is a Resume-Job Match Score?

When you apply for a job, one question always comes up: "Does my profile really match what the recruiter is looking for?" The match score provides an objective, quantified answer.

This score, expressed as a percentage, measures the fit between your resume content and the requirements of the targeted posting. The higher the score, the more your profile matches the recruiter's expectations — and the better your chances of landing an interview.

## How Is the Score Calculated?

The score calculation relies on several criteria analyzed by artificial intelligence:

### 1. Technical Skills Match

The algorithm compares the skills mentioned in your resume with those required in the posting. It goes beyond exact matching: it understands synonyms and variations (for example, "React.js" and "React" are recognized as identical).

### 2. Experience Fit

The requested experience level (junior, mid-level, senior) is compared with your background. The algorithm also evaluates the relevance of your past experiences to the target position.

### 3. ATS Keywords

Posting-specific keywords (technologies, methodologies, certifications) are identified and compared with your resume content. This is a critical factor because [ATS systems](/features) filter applications based on these keywords.

### 4. Structure and Format

Your resume structure is evaluated: sections present, information hierarchy, presentation clarity. A well-structured resume gets a better score because it's better parsed by ATS systems.

## Interpreting Your Score

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 90-100% | Excellent match — your profile is highly aligned with the posting |
| 75-89% | Good match — a few adjustments can improve your application |
| 60-74% | Average match — significant optimizations are recommended |
| Below 60% | Low match — the position requires skills your resume doesn't highlight |

A 100% score is neither realistic nor necessary. Aiming for a score between 80% and 90% is a reasonable goal that maximizes your chances while staying true to your background.

## How to Improve Your Score

### Use Targeted Suggestions

After the score calculation, AI provides concrete recommendations ranked by impact. For example:

- **High impact**: "Add testing tools (Jest, Cypress) mentioned in the posting"
- **Medium impact**: "Detail your agile methodology experience"
- **Low impact**: "Mention WCAG accessibility"

Each suggestion is actionable and can be applied in one click. [Discover the complete process →](/how-it-works)

### Rephrase Your Experiences

Sometimes the issue isn't a lack of skills but poor phrasing. "Team work" becomes "Scrum collaboration with 2-week sprints" — same experience, but much more impactful for an ATS.

### Add Missing Keywords

If the posting mentions a technology you know but your resume doesn't mention, that's an obvious improvement opportunity. AI identifies these gaps automatically.

## Score vs Reality: Limitations to Know

The match score is a decision-support tool, not a guarantee. Here's what it doesn't measure:

- **Your personality and soft skills** in real situations
- **Company culture** and your fit with the team
- **Your motivation** for the role
- **Unwritten criteria** the recruiter may have

Use the score as a guide to optimize your resume, not as an absolute criterion for deciding whether to apply. If a position genuinely interests you and your score is 65%, optimize what you can and apply anyway.

## The Score in Practice: A Concrete Example

Let's take a full-stack developer applying for a position requiring React, Node.js, and CI/CD experience.

**Initial score: 72%**
- Technical skills: 85% (React and Node.js mentioned)
- ATS keywords: 60% (CI/CD missing, "JavaScript" instead of "TypeScript")
- Experience: 75% (3 years vs. 3-5 years requested)

**After optimization: 87%**
- Added CI/CD to skills (they used GitHub Actions)
- Rephrased with TypeScript (they actually coded in TS)
- Detailed experience with quantified metrics

The score went from 72% to 87% without inventing anything — just better showcasing existing skills.

## Conclusion

The match score is a powerful tool for maximizing your interview chances. It gives you a clear picture of your positioning relative to a posting and guides you toward the most impactful improvements.
